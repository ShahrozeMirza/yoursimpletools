Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about solar in Pakistan

On-Grid connects directly to WAPDA and is the cheapest option but provides no backup during load shedding. Hybrid includes battery backup and works during load shedding making it the most popular choice in Pakistan. Off-Grid is fully independent from the grid with the highest upfront cost but complete energy independence.

A 1.5 ton inverter AC typically uses 1,200-1,500 watts. Running it for 8 hours during daytime requires approximately 10-12 kWh per day. This usually needs a 3-4 kW system depending on your total home load. Use the appliances tab above for a more accurate calculation based on all your appliances.

The net metering buyback rate was reduced to PKR 10 per unit for new consumers in 2026 compared to PKR 27 previously. This means exporting excess electricity earns very little. The smart approach is to size your system to match your daily consumption and maximize self-consumption. Even without net metering profits, savings of PKR 50-65 per unit make solar very worthwhile.

N-Type Monocrystalline or Bifacial panels from Tier-1 brands perform best in Pakistan. Longi, Jinko, JA Solar, Canadian Solar, and Trina are the most reliable brands. Look for panels with minimum 21% efficiency and a 25-year performance warranty. Higher wattage panels (580W-620W) reduce the number of panels needed and lower installation costs.

Most installations in Pakistan in 2026 use 580W to 620W panels. Higher wattage means fewer panels, less mounting structure, and often lower overall cost. Your dealer will confirm availability. Use the panel wattage your dealer quotes to get an accurate calculation from this tool.

For 4-6 hours of average home backup, most households need 2-4 batteries of 200Ah. Lithium batteries are more expensive but last longer, charge faster, and perform better. Tall Tubular lead-acid batteries are the budget option. Your load during load shedding hours determines the exact number needed.
